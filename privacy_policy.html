PRIVACY POLICY

Date: 01/04/2016

What we are

We want to depict the life of every location across the world as it happens. We are pursuing this vision by creating a platform where users are not people but locations. You become the location in which you are right now. Speaking for the location – You represent it on the world map and make it come to life. The responsibility lies with the community to maintain a healthy feed. Our motto – Be constructive, Be effective.

 

Report

Pole aspires to create a free and fair platform to represent a location. You can do your bit to keep your location feed clean. Report a spark/reply citing the reason. Our team shall review it. If content is found abusive, derogatory or malicious to a person or community - It shall be removed.The user reputation shall be penalized and account deactivated on repeated occurrences. Content you report from a Pole feed will be hidden from your feed immediately. However, Pole may maintain a copy of the content in our records. Mail us at spark@poletalks.com for any further reason/concern that might be mentioned in the report button.

 

Profile

Each user maintains his profile which has the sparks poled by him/her and the associated reputation points. This profile shows the credibility of the content you Pole and your reputation determines your visibility in the location feed algorithm.The reason a user cannot delete his/her spark is to deter people from spamming.

 

Invite Contacts

You can choose to "Share Pole" by inviting contacts via email, text or other means. You choose what contacts to invite and when to invite them. If you invite a contact, we will generate a message with the Pole download URL included, which you can choose to send via your mobile device.

 

Push Notifications/Alerts

We may send push notifications or alerts to your device.

Cookies

"Cookies" are small data file identifiers that are transferred to your computer that allow us to recognize your browser and transfer information about you and your use of our Services. For example, cookies that we use tell us how many times you have visited one of our Sites. We use "persistent" cookies to save your login information for future logins to the Services. We also use "session ID" cookies to enable certain features of the Services, to better understand how you interact with the Services and to monitor aggregate usage by users and web traffic routing on the Services. Unlike persistent cookies, session ID cookies are deleted from your computer when you log off from a Service and close your browser. You can instruct your browser, by changing its settings, to stop accepting cookies or to prompt you before accepting a cookie from our Sites. If you do not accept cookies, you may not be able to use all portions of the Services or all functionality of the Services.
